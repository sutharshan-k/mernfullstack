Greatest of All Time - Batters

No.1: Sachin Tendulkar

Debut: 1989

Runs: 34,357

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar, born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India, is widely regarded as the greatest batsman in the history of cricket. He holds records for the most runs in Tests (15,921) and ODIs (18,426), and he is the only cricketer with 100 international centuries. Known as the "Master Blaster", he made his debut in 1989 at age 16 and played until 2013. He was the cornerstone of India’s batting for two decades and a global ambassador of the sport. A key figure in India’s 2011 World Cup victory, Tendulkar was honored with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, upon retirement. His technique, temperament, and humility inspired millions around the world.

No.2: Kumar Sangakkara

Debut: 2000

Runs: 28,016

Kumar Sangakkara, born on October 27, 1977, in Sri Lanka, is celebrated as one of the most elegant left-handed batsmen and a top wicketkeeper. With over 28,000 runs in international cricket, he was the backbone of Sri Lankan batting through the 2000s. Sangakkara’s class and consistency were matched by his leadership, guiding Sri Lanka to the World Cup final in 2011. He holds the record for most double centuries by a Sri Lankan in Tests and had a brilliant partnership with Mahela Jayawardene. After retirement in 2015, he became a commentator, coach, and MCC President—continuing to contribute to the game globally.

No.3: Ricky Ponting

Debut: 1995

Runs: 27,483

Ricky Ponting, born on December 19, 1974, in Tasmania, is one of Australia's most decorated cricketers and captains. He scored over 27,000 international runs and led Australia to two consecutive World Cup titles (2003, 2007). Known for his fierce competitiveness and dominant pull shot, Ponting was a vital cog in Australia’s golden era. His Test and ODI centuries rank among the highest in the game, and his captaincy record is unmatched. After retiring in 2012, he took up coaching and commentary roles and has been a mentor in the IPL. Ponting’s legacy is defined by aggression, excellence, and an unrelenting desire to win.

No.4: Brian Lara

Debut: 1990

Runs: 26,345

Brian Lara, born May 2, 1969, in Trinidad, is considered one of the most stylish and explosive batsmen to ever play the game. Representing the West Indies from 1990 to 2007, Lara is best known for his world record 400* in Tests and 501* in first-class cricket. His footwork, flair, and ability to score quickly made him a nightmare for bowlers. Though he carried a struggling West Indies side, he still compiled 26,345 international runs. Lara received the Order of Australia and Trinidad's highest civilian award and has continued to support cricket through mentoring and charity work.

No.5: Jacques Kallis

Debut: 1995

Runs: 25,534

Jacques Kallis, born October 16, 1975, in Cape Town, is the most complete all-rounder of the modern era. With 25,534 international runs and 577 wickets, he combined rock-solid technique with effective medium-pace bowling. Kallis scored 45 Test centuries and averaged over 55 in Tests—remarkable for an all-rounder. He played a vital role in South Africa’s dominance in the early 2000s and was known for his calm, consistent performances. After retiring in 2014, he coached in domestic leagues and international setups, including roles with South Africa and IPL franchises. His quiet excellence remains a benchmark in world cricket.
